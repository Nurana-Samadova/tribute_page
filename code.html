Yuri Gagarin

Important years of Yuri Gagarin's life:

  • 1934 - born in Klushino, Smolensk Oblast, Russian SFSR, Soviet Union
  • 1955 - Starts attending First Chkalovsky Higher Air Force Pilots School, a flight school in Orenburg
  • 1957 - Starts flying solo,becomes a leutenant in the Soviet Air Forces, accumulates 166 hours and 47 minutes of flight time
  • 1959 - On 7 July 1959, he was rated Military Pilot 3rd Class. After expressing interest in space exploration following the launch of Luna 3 on 6 October 1959, his recommendation to the Soviet space programme was endorsed and forward by Lieutenant Colonel Babushkin. By this point, he had accumulated 265 hours of flight time. Gagarin was promoted to the rank of senior lieutenant on 6 November 1959, three weeks after he was interviewed by a medical commission for qualification to the space programme
  • 1960 - Gagarin starts trainings, such as physical exersises, parachute jumping, with other selected candidates and later on he is enrolled in a course programme for higher diploma in at the Zhukovsky Air Force Engineering Academy and earns his speciality degree only in 1968
  • 1961 - 12 April at 6:07 am UTC, the Vostok 3KA-3 (Vostok 1) spacecraft was launched from Baikonur Cosmodrome. Gagarin was the first human to travel into space, using the call sign Kedr
  • 1963 - He received a rank of colonel, then he became Deputy Training Director of the cosmonaut training facility
  • 1968 - While on a routine training flight from Chkalovsky Air Base, Gagarin and flight instructor Vladimir Seryogin died when their MiG-15UTI crashed near the town of Kirzhach. He died at the age of 34.

The date of Gagarin's space flight, 12 April, has been commemorated. Since 1962, it has been celebrated in the USSR and most of its former territories as Cosmonautics Day. Since 2000, Yuri's Night, an international celebration, is held annually to commemorate milestones in space exploration. In 2011, it was declared the International Day of Human Space Flight by the United Nations.
